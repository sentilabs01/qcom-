What is a DAO?

A Decentralized Autonomous Organization (DAO) is a community-run entity with no central authority, governed by transparent rules on a blockchain. QCom DAO uses this model to coordinate resources and accelerate quantum communication advancements.

Our DAO operates through smart contracts on the blockchain, enabling transparent, secure, and efficient governance. Token holders can participate in decision-making processes, propose initiatives, and vote on important matters affecting the future of quantum communication technology.

Quadratic Voting System

QCom DAO uses quadratic voting to ensure fair representation and prevent domination by large token holders. Voting power scales with the square root of tokens staked, not linearly.

This innovative approach balances influence across the community, allowing smaller token holders to have meaningful impact while still rewarding larger stakeholders.

Key Benefits:

  • Prevents plutocracy (rule by the wealthy)
  • Encourages broader participation
  • Rewards conviction and commitment
  • Aligns with decentralization principles

In quadratic voting, someone with 10,000 tokens has 100x (not 10,000x) the voting power of someone with 1 token.
